About Me

I'm a student and maker based in Beirut. I focus on embedded systems, wireless protocols, and the space where hardware meets security. I build real things: bare-metal firmware, RF tools, and open-source projects that people actually use.

I spend a lot of hours buried in datasheets or debugging with a logic analyzer. I enjoy reverse engineering gadgets and building autonomous robotics. If it has a signal or a low level protocol, I want to understand how it works and how to make it do more.

Outside the lab, I've worked as a technical instructor for rappelling and caving. It taught me to stay calm and solve problems when things get difficult. I'm just here to build cool stuff and see what happens when you push a system to its limit.
